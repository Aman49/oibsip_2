Srinivas Ramanujan

Introduction

Srinivas Ramanujan was an Indian mathematician who made significant contributions to the field of mathematics, particularly in the areas of number theory and infinite series. Born in 1887 in Erode, Tamil Nadu, Ramanujan was largely self-taught and had an innate talent for mathematics.

Achievements

  • Discovered the Ramanujan prime and the Ramanujan theta function
  • Developed the Ramanujan-Sato series
  • Contributed to the development of the Hardy-Ramanujan-Littlewood circle method
  • Discovered several new identities and equations in number theory

Legacy

Ramanujan's work has had a profound impact on mathematics and continues to inspire mathematicians today. He was elected a Fellow of the Royal Society in 1918 and was awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, in 1954. In 2012, the Government of India declared December 22, Ramanujan's birthday, as National Mathematics Day.
